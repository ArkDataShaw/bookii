Security

Practices as of July 2026 · honest about our stage

Encryption

TLS everywhere in transit. Calendar OAuth tokens and connected-account credentials are encrypted at rest with AES-256-GCM using keys held outside the database. Passwords are stored only as salted scrypt hashes.

Least access

Calendar connections request the narrowest scopes that work: free/busy reads for availability and event-write for the bookings we create. We never read your event titles, descriptions, or attendees for availability. Databases and caches bind to localhost; the only public surfaces are the website, the app, and the documented API.

Booking integrity

Double-bookings are prevented at the database layer (exclusion constraints), not just in application code. All booking mutations support idempotency keys.

Infrastructure

API and database on Google Cloud Platform (us-east1); static site and app on Netlify's CDN. Access to production is limited to authorized operators over key-based SSH. See our subprocessors list.

What we don't have yet

We're early, and pretending otherwise would be a red flag: no SOC 2 audit yet, no bug-bounty program (disclosure is welcome, see below), single-region infrastructure. These graduate onto the roadmap as the product grows.

Responsible disclosure

Found a vulnerability? Email security@bookii.to (also published at /.well-known/security.txt). We aim to acknowledge within 3 business days. Good-faith research against your own account is welcome; don't access other users' data, degrade the service, or run automated scanners against production.
