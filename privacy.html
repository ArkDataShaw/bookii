Privacy Policy

Effective July 21, 2026 · Bookii ("Bookii," "we," "us")

Bookii is a calendar booking service that lets people ("hosts") share availability and lets others ("invitees") book time with them. This policy explains what we collect, why, and the control you have over it. The short version: we collect only what scheduling requires, we never sell your data, and calendar data is used solely to compute availability and manage bookings.

1. Information we collect

Account data (hosts). Name, email address, password (stored only as a salted hash), username, timezone, and the settings you configure (event types, schedules, booking questions).

Booking data (invitees). When you book a meeting: your name, email address, answers to the host's booking questions, the meeting time, and your timezone. Invitees do not need an account.

Calendar data (hosts who connect a calendar). See section 2.

Technical data. Standard server logs (IP address, browser type, pages requested) used for security and reliability. We do not run third-party advertising or cross-site tracking, and we do not use non-essential cookies. Authentication uses a token stored in your browser's local storage.

2. Google user data

If you connect a Google account, Bookii requests these Google Calendar scopes:

  • calendar.freebusy — to read only free/busy intervals from your calendars, so booked and busy times are removed from your public availability. We do not read event titles, descriptions, attendees, or other event details through this scope.
  • calendar.events — to create a calendar event on your chosen destination calendar when someone books you, to update it when a booking changes, and to delete it when a booking is cancelled. We only write and manage events that Bookii itself created.
  • openid email — to identify which Google account you connected.

How we use it. Google Calendar data is used exclusively to (a) compute your bookable availability and (b) create, update, and delete booking events on your behalf. It is never used for advertising, profiling, or model training, and is never sold or shared with third parties.

How we store it. OAuth access and refresh tokens are encrypted at rest (AES-256-GCM) on our servers. Free/busy intervals are held in a short-lived in-memory cache (about 60 seconds) to render booking pages quickly and are not persisted to our database.

How you revoke it. Disconnect the calendar in the app (Calendars → Disconnect) — we revoke the token with Google and delete our stored copies — or revoke Bookii's access at myaccount.google.com/permissions.

Bookii's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

3. Other connected calendars

Microsoft and Apple calendar connections (when available) follow the same rules as section 2: free/busy reads for availability, event writes for bookings, encrypted credential storage, revocable at any time.

4. How we use information

  • Operate the service: compute availability, create bookings, send booking-related notifications (confirmations, reminders, cancellations, reschedules).
  • Secure the service: prevent abuse, rate-limit, debug failures.
  • Communicate with hosts about their account. We do not send marketing email to invitees.

5. Sharing

We share data only with the infrastructure providers needed to run Bookii (hosting: Google Cloud Platform; static hosting/CDN: Netlify), with a host and their invitee sharing each other's booking details as inherent to scheduling a meeting, and where required by law. We do not sell personal information and we do not share it for advertising.

6. Retention & deletion

Account data is kept while your account is active. Deleting your account removes your profile, event types, schedules, connected-calendar credentials, and bookings from our systems. Invitees may cancel a booking at any time via the link in their confirmation; cancelled bookings are retained in the host's meeting history unless the host's account is deleted. To request deletion of your data as an invitee, contact us at the address below.

7. Your rights

Depending on where you live (including under GDPR and CCPA), you may have rights to access, correct, export, or delete your personal data, and to object to or restrict processing. Contact us and we will honor verified requests within 30 days.

8. Security

Transport encryption (TLS) everywhere; OAuth tokens encrypted at rest; passwords stored as salted scrypt hashes; least-scope calendar access; access to production systems restricted to authorized operators.

9. Children

Bookii is not directed to children under 16 and we do not knowingly collect their data.

10. Changes

We will post any changes here and update the effective date. Material changes affecting Google user data handling will be reflected in this policy before they take effect.

11. Contact

Questions or data requests: privacy@bookii.to
