Data Processing Addendum

Effective July 21, 2026 · incorporated into the Terms of Service

This DPA applies automatically, without signature, to every customer whose use of Bookii involves personal data subject to the GDPR, UK GDPR, or similar laws. If your procurement process requires a countersigned copy, email privacy@bookii.to.

1. Roles

For invitee and booking data processed on your behalf, you are the controller and Bookii is the processor. For your own account data, Bookii is an independent controller as described in the Privacy Policy.

2. Processing details (Art. 28(3))

Subject matter & duration: scheduling services, for the term of your account. Nature & purpose: computing availability, creating and managing bookings, sending booking notifications. Data categories: invitee names, email addresses, booking question answers, meeting times, timezones; hosts' calendar free/busy intervals. Data subjects: your invitees and calendar attendees.

3. Our obligations

We will: process personal data only on your documented instructions (using the service is the instruction); ensure persons processing data are bound by confidentiality; implement the technical and organisational measures described on our security page; assist you, as reasonably possible, with data-subject requests and Art. 32–36 obligations; delete or return personal data on account deletion; and make available the information necessary to demonstrate compliance.

4. Subprocessors (Art. 28(2))

You grant general authorisation for the subprocessors listed at bookii.to/subprocessors. We'll update that page before adding any; objections may be raised within 14 days of a change, and if we can't resolve an objection you may terminate and we'll delete your data.

5. International transfers

Data is processed in the United States. For transfers from the EEA/UK, the EU Standard Contractual Clauses (Module 2, controller→processor) are incorporated by reference, with you as data exporter and Bookii as data importer.

6. Breach notification

We will notify you without undue delay after becoming aware of a personal data breach affecting your data, with the information reasonably required for your own notifications.

7. Liability

Liability under this DPA is subject to the limitations in the Terms of Service.
